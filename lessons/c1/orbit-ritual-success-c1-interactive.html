1 • Ritual or procedure?

Choose the exact noun.

The tea ceremony is a cultural ______ that has been preserved for centuries.

ritual is a traditional series of actions linked to culture or religion. A procedure is a practical set of official steps: a safety procedure, an application procedure.
2 • Grammar frame

Type the missing form.

The host begins each item needed for the ceremony before arranging it carefully. GATHER

begin by + -ing. The correct answer is by gathering - meaning alone is not enough; the frame decides it.
3 • Mars word formation

Form the word that fits.

1. Conditions on Mars are extremely . FIT
2. The experiment took place in a small space. RESTRICT
3. They studied the effect of . ISOLATE
4. The lack of privacy affected social . INTERACT

Key: unfitting; restricted; isolation; interactions.
Look for sentence signals: extremely needs an adjective; effect of needs a noun; social needs a plural noun here.
4 • Collocation checkpoint

Which verb belongs here?

The research team had to ______ a series of controlled experiments.

conduct an experiment / research / an investigation. You operate a machine, practise a skill and follow instructions. This is a collocation test, not a synonym test.
5 • Noun suffixes

Tap the outsider.

Which word does not use the same noun suffix?

refusal is the odd one out: -al. The others use -ness. Say the base words aloud: aware, dark, kind, refuse.
6 • Abstract nouns

Do not choose an ending because it sounds formal. First identify the meaning and grammatical role; then select a real word from that family.
7 • The price of success

Word formation under pressure.

Key: achievements; jealous; ability; pursuit; relentlessly; exhaustion.

8 • Transformation lab

Rebuild the chunk.

1. My manager never seems to appreciate my work.
GRANTED

2. The new performance was much better than last week’s.
CONSIDERABLY

Key: takes me for granted; considerably better than.
Transformations are about reconstructing a natural phrase around the fixed word, not translating every word mechanically.
9 • Transformation lab

Two more patterns.

3. Daniela completed the task efficiently.
CARRIED
Daniela efficiently.

4. Without Selina’s support, I could not have stayed in the job.
BEEN
If it Selina’s support, I could not have stayed in the job.

Key: carried out the task; had not been for.
carry out needs an object. If it had not been for… introduces a past unreal condition.
10 • Final precision test

Which sentence survives?

have an influence on is the complete chunk. The wrong options are close enough to be tempting - which is why they are good C1 distractors.
11 • Speaking mission

Make a case, not a word list.

“Elite sport often demands a personal sacrifice that is not healthy.”

Speak for 90 seconds. Refer to the Mars mission or the athlete text. Make one precise distinction: discipline vs obsession, isolation vs focus, or achievement vs personal cost.

Use at least three: the pursuit of • conduct research • have an influence on • be envious of • take somebody for granted
