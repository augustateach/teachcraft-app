C1+ revision • Edison to the Paralympian

From Edison to the Paralympian

A C1+ precision revision built from your recent classwork and homework: Edison, museum modernisation, personality vocabulary and key word transformations.

Mission: stop choosing a word because it feels broadly right. Choose it because the sentence makes it inevitable.
1 • The diagnostic

Why did the answers go wrong?

Meaning

conceited ≠ impetuous

One is about an inflated opinion of yourself; the other is about acting without thought.

Grammar frame

never been defeated

The passive pattern decides the answer before the general meaning does.

Collocation

held in high regard

English stores many C1 phrases as a whole chunk.

Register

of utmost importance

Precise upgrades need to sound natural, not merely impressive.

2 • Meaning first

Choose the accusation.

Having never been defeated in a race, she was sometimes accused of being ______.

conceited = excessively proud of your abilities. Impetuous describes rash action; headstrong means determined to do what you want; upbeat means optimistic.
3 • Grammar frame decides

Read the whole structure.

Having never been ______ in a race, I admit I should have been a little more modest.

defeated is required by the passive form: be defeated in a race. You can feel triumphant after winning, but you cannot be “triumphant in a race” in this structure.
4 • Character under pressure

Which word completes both logic and tone?

I was angry at myself for being so ______ and taking such unnecessary risks.

impetuous means acting suddenly, without considering consequences. The phrase taking unnecessary risks is the decisive clue.
5 • Near-neighbours

Sort the personality vocabulary.

Say the contrast aloud before you look at the key.

gutsy
brave and willing to take risks, especially in a difficult situation
cautious
careful to avoid danger, mistakes or unnecessary risk
headstrong
determined to do what you want, even when others advise against it
apprehensive
worried about what may happen
upbeat
cheerful and optimistic, often despite difficulties
modest
not talking proudly about your achievements
Fast test: “You’ll never get anywhere if you’re so cautious all the time.” Being gutsy might be valuable; being too cautious is presented as the obstacle.
6 • Precision battle

Which sentence is genuinely strong?

of utmost importance is a formal fixed phrase. Use it for serious significance. Crucial and vital are often cleaner choices when you need an adjective: a crucial decision / vital funding.
7 • Upgrade, don’t inflate

Choose the only natural upgrade.

The committee identified a ______ flaw in the proposal.

a crucial flaw is natural. Utmost usually appears in of utmost importance / with the utmost care. Vitality is a noun meaning energy.
8 • Word family: museum

Make the form obey the sentence.

Complete mentally, then reveal.

17. the world’s most important and ______ cultural showcases. INNOVATE
18. The collections are absolutely ______. STAND
19. the shape and order of modern societies / ______. CIVILISE
20. had these a(n) ______ impact. SIGNIFY
21. has ______ an amazing makeover. GO
22. The new layout ______ people to appreciate… ABLE
23. fully ______ to everyone. ACCESS
24. so ______ to all those involved. CONGRATULATE
Key: innovative; outstanding; civilisations; significant; undergone; enables; accessible; congratulations.
9 • Word formation: the trap

Ask four questions before you write.

1. What part of speech?

an amazing ___ → noun: makeover

2. Singular or plural?

modern ___ → plural countable noun: civilisations

3. Active or passive?

has ___ → past participle: undergone

4. Which preposition/chunk?

congratulations to, not congratulatory to

10 • Abstract nouns

The noun tells you what kind of meaning is needed.

1. The project needs greater ______, not merely another attractive design. ADAPT
2. His greatest ______ was finishing the expedition despite the injury. ACCOMPLISH
3. The public’s growing ______ of the issue changed the debate. REALISE
4. She spoke with remarkable ______, despite being under pressure. DETERMINE

Key: adaptability; accomplishment; realisation; determination.
Watch it: adaptation is a particular change made to something; adaptability is the ability to adjust.
11 • Collocation surgery

Which transformation is exact?

Dr Ramesh’s colleagues respected him highly.

be held in high regard = be highly respected. Option B reverses the meaning: it says he respected them.
12 • Key word transformations

Build chunks, not word salad.

Use 3–6 words, including the word given. Do not change the word.

25. Whether you present on Thursday or Friday makes no difference to the other students.
DIFFERENCE
The other students do not mind ______ your presentation.
26. “What are you thinking of doing for the college’s centenary celebration?”
MIND
The tutor asked the students what ______ for the college’s centenary celebration.
27. Dr Ramesh’s colleagues respected him highly.
HELD
Dr Ramesh ______ by his colleagues.
Suggested answers: whether you give; they had in mind; was held in high regard.
13 • Transformation traps

Finish the remaining set.

28. Learning new languages had never been a problem for her.
COME
Learning new languages had always ______ her.
29. The call centre gave us incorrect train times, apparently.
MISINFORMED
We seem ______ about train times.
30. Her party is unlikely to win the election.
EVENT
In the ______, she’ll become President.
Key: come easily to; to have been misinformed; unlikely event of her party winning.
14 • Materials & technology

Definition is a precision test.

kiln
a very hot oven used to bake clay, bricks or pottery
agility
the ability to move or think quickly and effectively
lunatic
a person who behaves in a wildly unreasonable way
hardback
a book with a stiff, durable cover
MDF
a manufactured board made from compressed wood fibres and resin
reinforced
made stronger by adding extra material or support
nickel
a hard, silver-coloured metal used in coins and batteries
15 • C1 speaking mission

Defend a choice under pressure.

Prompt: A young athlete is determined to compete despite a serious setback. Is that person gutsy, headstrong, impetuous, or all three? Decide, then justify your choice with two precise distinctions.

Use at least three: be held in high regard • of utmost importance • take unnecessary risks • be apprehensive about • an outstanding accomplishment

90 seconds

Teacher lens: does the student distinguish the words, or merely list them?
